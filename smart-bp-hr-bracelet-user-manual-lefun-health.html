So how does a smart bracelet measure blood pressure?

Many fitness trackers take oscillometric readings in the morning and evening. This automatic blood pressure measurement consists of an electronic pressure sensor that calculates estimates of systolic and diastolic pressures. Other fitness trackers with blood pressure monitors use a PPG or photoplethysmogram.

A PPG is a heart rate sensor that uses a pulse oximeter. This sensor illuminates the skin and measures any changes in light absorption. Besides, it also uses ECG readings that work algorithms. However, they don’t have an inflatable cuff, and readings are not as accurate as the sphygmomanometer which is used at the doctor’s office. Pros

  • Helps you manage or prevent high blood pressure or other health problems

  • Many fitness watches with blood pressure also track heart rate variability which is the body’s reaction in the time interval between heartbeats which gives you a more clear picture of your heart health.

The best way to know if a fitness tracker with BP is accurate

Do you want to know if your smartwatch with the blood pressure tracker is accurate enough? The best way to check if your device is accurate is to bring it to the doctor’s office. Measure your blood pressure with your fitness watch and compare your doctors’ readings. However, because blood pressure is not an absolute number results may be approximate or close enough.

However, keep in mind that blood pressure changes during the day depending on the weather, food that you eat, genetics, age, weight, mood, medication, physical activity, stress levels, and alcohol. Blood pressure can be lower at night and will rise before you wake up. It will continue to rise during the day picking up in the afternoon.

When you measure your blood pressure either at the doctor’s office or with a fitness tracker we are looking for any changes or patterns. You may discover that eating certain foods or doing specific physical activity may increase or decrease your blood pressure.
